Dal Chawal Recipe

Description

Dal Chawal, a quintessential Indian dish, brings together the simplicity of lentils with aromatic basmati rice. This comfort food is not only nutritious but also boasts a symphony of spices that elevate its flavor profile. The lentils, cooked to perfection, are tempered with spices and served over steamed basmati rice, making Dal Chawal a hearty and satisfying meal for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup split yellow dal (lentils)
  • 1 cup Basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Steps

  1. Rinse the lentils and cook them in a pot until soft. Set aside.
  2. In a separate pot, heat ghee or oil. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Stir in ginger-garlic paste and green chili. Sauté for a couple of minutes.
  5. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
  6. Introduce turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Add the cooked lentils to the pot and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  8. Cook basmati rice until fluffy.
  9. Serve the dal over steamed rice. Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  10. Enjoy a comforting bowl of Dal Chawal, a timeless Indian classic!
